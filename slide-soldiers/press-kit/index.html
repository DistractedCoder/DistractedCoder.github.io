At a Glance

It’s a new twist on sliding block puzzles! It’s not about reaching the goal, just hitting the targets.

Plan your next move because once you choose a direction, you’ll keep moving until you hit a wall.

  • 4,000 Levels - Slide through 4,000 pre-generated levels ranging from easy to hard!
  • Fully Hand-drawn - Fully hand-drawn by real human hands! No AI was used.
  • Find the Path - Every level has a perfect solution. Will you find it?
  • Grid Based - Always see where you are going and always end up where you’d expect.
  • Play at your own pace - No timer and relaxed gameplay keeps things chill.
  • No randomness - Every outcome is fully predictable. If you get lost, that’s on you!

Features

  • Fast sliding movement means commitment. Once you choose a direction, you’ll keep moving until you hit a wall.
  • It’s not about reaching the goal or covering all tiles but rather striking the targets. This is not your typical sliding puzzle.
  • Adventure Mode limits visibility, asking players to remember their path and follow their trail through dense forests. Levels are built without dead ends, so progress always comes from understanding, not trial and error.
  • Hard Mode introduces false enemies placed just out of reach, designed to mislead players who rush their decisions.
  • Hand-drawn art makes everything pop, even as puzzles grow more challenging.
  • A relaxing soundtrack supports focus without distractions.
  • Seasonal changes transform the forest, keeping familiar mechanics visually fresh as you play.

Who We Are

Distracted Coder is a solo independent game studio based just outside of Chicago.

I'm Alex Wagner and I'm the founder and developer behind the studio. I handle the game design, programming, and creative direction for every project.

How it Started

I have been coding and making games since I was in 6th grade. It's been 15 years now!

I released my first iOS game at 19, which went on to surpass a few thousand downloads. After releasing 2 more apps and spending five years creating content on YouTube, I set my sights on something bigger: a Steam game, and maybe someday consoles.
